# Interview Framework - Capturing Paul Richards' Voice

**Purpose:** Systematically capture Paul's expertise, stories, opinions, and unique perspective to ensure the book sounds authentically like Paul Richards, not generic AI-generated content.

---

## Interview Structure

Each interview session focuses on a specific topic area. Sessions are designed to be conversational, capturing both technical knowledge and personal stories.

### Session Format
1. **Opening context** - What we're exploring today
2. **Core questions** - Deep dive into the topic
3. **Story prompts** - Real experiences and examples
4. **Opinion questions** - Paul's unique take
5. **Audience translation** - How to explain this to our readers

---

## Interview Sessions

### Session 1: Origin Story & Vision
**Goal:** Capture Paul's journey and why this book needs to exist

**Questions:**
1. Tell me about your path from [early career] to Chief Streaming Officer. What were the pivotal moments?
2. When did you first realize AI was going to transform broadcast/ProAV?
3. What was your first hands-on experience with visual AI? What surprised you?
4. Why write this book now? What's the urgency?
5. Who do you picture reading this book? Give me a specific person.
6. What do you want readers to feel after finishing this book?
7. What misconceptions about AI in broadcast frustrate you most?
8. Complete this sentence: "If broadcast professionals don't understand visual reasoning in the next 2-3 years, they will..."

**Story Prompts:**
- Tell me about a time AI solved a problem you thought was unsolvable
- Describe a production disaster that could have been prevented with visual reasoning
- Share a moment when you saw someone "get it" about AI

---

### Session 2: The AI Landscape (Foundations)
**Goal:** Capture Paul's practical understanding of AI concepts

**Questions:**
1. How do you explain AI to a skeptical broadcast engineer in 60 seconds?
2. What's the most common misunderstanding about LLMs you encounter?
3. When you first saw a VLM in action, what clicked for you?
4. Why do you think visual reasoning is the breakthrough for our industry specifically?
5. How do you decide between cloud and local models for a project?
6. What's your take on API costs? How do you frame this for budget conversations?
7. What tools are you personally excited about right now?
8. What's overhyped? What's underhyped?

**Story Prompts:**
- Describe demonstrating AI to someone who was initially skeptical
- Tell me about a project where you chose local over cloud (or vice versa) and why
- Share a time when the AI did something that genuinely surprised you

---

### Session 3: Visual Understanding Deep Dive
**Goal:** Capture technical insights and practical wisdom

**Questions:**
1. Walk me through how you approach a new visual reasoning project
2. What makes a good prompt for visual tasks? What makes a bad one?
3. How do you think about confidence scores? What thresholds do you use?
4. What are the failure modes you've learned to watch for?
5. How do you explain "semantic understanding" vs pixel detection to a TD?
6. What's the hardest part of object tracking that people underestimate?
7. How do you test visual reasoning systems before going live?

**Story Prompts:**
- Walk me through a project from problem to solution
- Describe a time when prompt engineering made the difference
- Tell me about a visual reasoning failure and what you learned

---

### Session 4: Production Integration
**Goal:** Capture real-world implementation wisdom

**Questions:**
1. What's your philosophy on AI + human control in live production?
2. How do you think about safety and fail-safes?
3. What's your approach to vMix integration specifically?
4. How do you handle latency requirements in live environments?
5. What should never be fully automated?
6. How do you train production teams to work alongside AI?
7. What's your testing process before a live show?

**Story Prompts:**
- Tell me about the first time you used AI in a live production
- Describe a close call or near-disaster and how it was handled
- Share a success story where AI made the impossible possible

---

### Session 5: Industry Applications
**Goal:** Capture sector-specific insights

**Questions (Sports):**
1. What makes sports broadcasting uniquely suited for visual reasoning?
2. Walk me through the scoreboard extraction problem
3. How do you handle different sports with different visual layouts?
4. What's the future of AI in sports production?

**Questions (Worship):**
1. What are the unique sensitivities in worship production?
2. How do you approach automation in a worship context?
3. What applications have you seen work well?

**Questions (Education):**
1. How is educational streaming different from other verticals?
2. What visual reasoning applications make sense for classrooms?
3. What are the privacy considerations?

**Questions (Corporate):**
1. What do enterprise clients care about most?
2. How do you position AI for corporate AV teams?

**Story Prompts:**
- Share a memorable sports production AI moment
- Tell me about a worship tech team's reaction to visual AI
- Describe an educational use case that excited you

---

### Session 6: Multimodal & Advanced Systems
**Goal:** Capture thinking on audio integration and system architecture

**Questions:**
1. When did you realize audio + visual was the next step?
2. How do you think about combining confidence from different modalities?
3. What's your architecture philosophy for complex systems?
4. How do you approach model swapping and future-proofing?
5. What does "production-ready" mean to you?
6. What's in your logging and observability toolkit?

**Story Prompts:**
- Describe building a multimodal system
- Tell me about a debugging nightmare and how you solved it
- Share a system that you're particularly proud of architecturally

---

### Session 7: The Future & Industry Impact
**Goal:** Capture forward-looking perspective and vision

**Questions:**
1. Where is visual reasoning in broadcast in 5 years?
2. What capabilities are you waiting for?
3. How will job roles change?
4. What advice do you have for someone starting in this field today?
5. What's your biggest concern about AI in broadcast?
6. What's your biggest hope?
7. If you could tell the entire broadcast industry one thing about AI, what would it be?

**Story Prompts:**
- Describe the production of the future
- Tell me about a young professional who gives you hope
- Share your vision for PTZOptics and StreamGeeks in the AI era

---

### Session 8: Writing Voice & Style
**Goal:** Capture how Paul communicates

**Questions:**
1. How would you describe your communication style?
2. What other authors/speakers do you admire? What do you like about them?
3. Do you prefer technical depth or accessible overview?
4. How do you use humor in professional communication?
5. What phrases or expressions do you naturally use?
6. What writing style turns you off?
7. Read this paragraph back - does it sound like you?

**Exercises:**
- Explain VLMs to me like I'm a skeptical 50-year-old TD
- Explain the same thing to a curious 25-year-old streaming enthusiast
- Give me your "elevator pitch" for visual reasoning

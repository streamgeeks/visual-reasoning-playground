[1 INTRODUCTION]{.smallcaps}

Imagine sitting down in your studio, cameras ready, lights set just
right---and instead of scrambling to manage every detail, you've got an
AI-powered assistant working alongside you. It's helping you switch
cameras, generate captions, improve your lighting, even write your
scripts. That's the world we're stepping into together.

This book and online course is designed to be your guided tour through
the exciting intersection of artificial intelligence and video
production. Whether you're a solo creator streaming from your home
office, a church tech volunteer running Sunday services, or part of a
professional broadcast team, AI is rapidly becoming a valuable tool to
make life easier and our jobs more productive.

Now, let's pause for a moment. Because for many of us, technology often
feels a little like magic. Think about the calculator---you don't *need*
one to do math, but the time it saves is undeniable. You probably don't
know how every chip inside is assembled, but over time, you learn to
trust the device because it consistently gives you accurate results. AI
is similar to the calculator in some ways. You don't have to be an
engineer to benefit from it. But it *does* help to understand the big
picture: how computer vision sees the world, how large language models
(LLMs) generate text, and how automation stitches workflows together.
With just that foundational knowledge, you gain the confidence to get
creative---and that's when the real magic happens. Suddenly, you're not
just consuming technology, you're using it to design new and inspiring
workflows for yourself and your customers.

**Node-Based Thinking**

One of the themes you'll see repeated throughout this book is the
importance of **"node-based" thinking.** Rather than subscribing to an
AI service that hides everything behind the curtain---where the process
feels like magic you can't quite touch---node-based AI workflows lay the
entire flow out in front of you. Imagine a visual map where each piece
of the process is represented by a node: one for computer vision,
another for language generation, another for automation. You can
connect, rearrange, and swap these nodes like puzzle pieces, building a
custom workflow that's uniquely yours. This approach not only fuels
creativity but also deepens your understanding of how AI works. You're
no longer locked into a "black box" system; you can tinker, test, and
customize. And that ability to see under the hood is what unlocks
entirely new possibilities for your projects and your customers.

If you've ever worked with a video switcher or an audio mixing board,
you already understand the power of nodes---even if you didn't call them
that. Each input channel, effect, and output is essentially a "node" in
your production chain. You can trace how a microphone feeds into a
compressor, through EQ, and out to your live stream. Or how a camera
source flows into a switcher, through a graphics layer, and finally to
your broadcast. In the same way, node-based AI workflows let you *see
the path your data takes.* Instead of guessing what's happening behind
the scenes, you can visually map the flow: video feeds passing through
computer vision models, text prompts moving into language models, and
automation triggers kicking off at the right moment. For video
professionals, this way of thinking feels intuitive---it's like patching
signals through a routing grid, but instead of only moving video and
audio, you're shaping intelligence itself.

  adjustments.

- Using AI for real-time captions and translations.

- Streamlining pre-production tasks like scriptwriting, storyboarding,
  and shot planning.

Throughout the course, you will learn both the "how" and the "why." The
goal isn't to replace your creativity---it's to amplify it. By
offloading repetitive tasks to AI, you'll have more time and focus to
craft the moments that really matter.

Think of this as both a roadmap and a toolkit. By the end, you'll walk
away confident in using AI to save time, improve quality, and bring
fresh creative energy into your productions.
